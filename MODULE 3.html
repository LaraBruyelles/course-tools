Agency Non-Negotiables

These come straight from top cruise agencies. Every item below is a reason a reel gets rejected — or hired.

🚫 Fatal Mistakes — any one of these will get your reel rejected

✕
Private, password-protected, or broken link. If an agent can't open it in 10 seconds, it goes in the bin.
✕
Same genre / same energy for every clip. Even if you play it brilliantly — 10 ballads in a row says "I can only do one thing".
✕
Studio-only footage with no audience. A technically perfect recording in an empty room does not prove you can hold a crowd on a ship for 4 hours.
✕
Long MC intros or dead time at the start. If the music doesn't start in the first 5 seconds, most agents will move on.
✕
Prioritising production quality over live energy. A shaky phone video of you genuinely connecting with an audience will always beat a studio-shot reel with no crowd.

Filming Your Performances

Audience footage is the single most important raw material for your reel. Everything else — editing, audio quality, production — comes second. Get in front of people and film.

📌 Tied to your Module 1 commitment

In Module 1 you committed to booking gigs and open mics. This is where that commitment becomes footage. Every performance you play from now on is a filming opportunity — treat it that way.

1

Open mic nights — go as often as possible

The fastest, cheapest way to get live audience footage. Most cities have multiple open mics per week. You don't need to be booked — just sign up on the night. Film every single one.

2

Restaurants, bars, cafés, hotel lobbies

Call ahead, ask to play a short set — even for free or reduced rate. Ambient background gigs still count as live audience footage. Hotel lobbies especially love live music.

3

Retirement homes and care facilities

Consistently receptive, often grateful, and the audience is very real. These bookings are easy to arrange, repeat, and the repertoire overlap with cruise ship is high.

4

Busking (where legal)

Immediate audience, zero gatekeeping. Check local licensing rules. A phone + tripod on a busy high street generates authentic crowd footage quickly — people stopping to listen is gold.

5

Friends', community events, private parties

Birthdays, house parties, community gatherings. Offer to play. These audiences are naturally engaged and easy to film. Don't be precious — any real people in the room counts.

6

Last resort: staged "live-feel" session with invited audience

Invite 10–20 friends or fellow musicians to watch you perform. Set up in a venue or large room. It must feel and look like a real performance — not a rehearsal. Only use this if you genuinely cannot access any of the above options.

💡 Film everything — sort it later

Hit record before you start and don't stop until the set ends. You'll have a 2-hour file — that's fine. Scrub through it afterwards to find the best 15–30 second clips. Natural moments and real reactions are what you're looking for. A phone video of a genuinely engaged crowd beats a studio recording every single time.

⏰ Critical reminder

Set a recurring Sunday reminder to review your progress. Make sure you have at least 1 month before your application deadline with all footage captured, edited, and uploaded. Don't leave this to the last minute.

Your reel stays the same. But knowing the personality of each line lets you pitch yourself as the right fit — not just a good musician. Agents like Ash at Blackburn pitch specific performers to specific lines: "I can't wait to show Jeff at Celebrity this person." That's the level of targeting you're aiming for.

Ultra-Luxury
Seabourn · Azamara · Regent

Jazz, standards, sophisticated contemporary. Smaller ships, intimate lounges, discerning guests. Your reel should lead with elegance and versatility.

Premium
Celebrity · Holland America · Princess

Broad appeal — pop, rock, standards mix. Evening entertainment focus, mixed age groups. Range and crowd connection matter most.

Contemporary / Mass Market
Carnival · Royal Caribbean · Norwegian

High energy, contemporary pop, accessible catalog. Younger mixed demographic. Your reel should show energy, uptempo range, and audience interaction.

European / Language Lines
TUI · AIDA · MSC

Language is a filter before your reel is even watched. German lines want German speakers. If you have relevant language skills or European repertoire, lead with it.

⚠️ The international repertoire test

Cruise ships carry guests from dozens of countries. Before putting any song in your reel or set list, ask: "Would a passenger from Japan, Germany, and the US all potentially recognise this?" If the answer is no — regional hits, local artists, national-market-only songs — it's a risk unless you're specifically targeting a line that caters to that market.
