Before You Build Your List

Most musicians jump straight to learning songs. The ones who get hired ask better questions first. This takes 10 minutes and will save you months of learning the wrong material.

🌍 The International Recognition Test

Your audience comes from dozens of countries — Japan, Germany, the US, Australia, Brazil — all in the same room. A French chanson or a national hit that your home country loves is a risk unless the line specifically targets that market.

For every song on your list, ask yourself:

"Would a passenger from Japan, Germany, and the USA all potentially recognise this song?"

If the answer is no — or maybe — it probably shouldn't be in your core repertoire.

Beyond the Songs: Interactive Sets That Get You Re-Booked

Sourced directly from a major cruise agency — this is the differentiator most musicians ignore.

🚢 Why This Matters

World cruises and long itineraries can have 10–12 sea days in a single two-week voyage. That's a lot of time to fill — and Cruise Directors are actively looking for musicians who can do more than just perform.

A musician who can run an interactive set makes the CD's life easier, fills programming gaps effortlessly, and becomes someone they want to re-book. It's one of the most reliable ways to go from a one-contract hire to a recurring artist.

🌍 Bonus: it works brilliantly for international audiences. When half your crowd's first language isn't English, a sing-along or name-that-tune doesn't need perfect verbal communication — the music does the work.

🎹 The Formats to Prepare

🎵
Name That Tune

Play the intro of a recognisable song and let the audience guess. Works for all ages, all nationalities. Keep a list of 30–50 crowd-pleasers ready.

🎲
Musical Bingo

Passengers get bingo cards with song titles. You play the intros — they mark them off. Simple, hilarious, and completely repeatable with different card sets.

🎤
Trivia & Quiz Sets

You play live while hosting a music trivia quiz — who sang this? What year? Name the film. The piano makes every answer more dramatic. Works well for themed evenings (Motown, 80s, film music).

🎶
Sing-Alongs

Classic crowd participation. Hand out lyric sheets or put lyrics on screen. Works best with timeless songs everyone knows — Beatles, Abba, classic musicals. Sea-day gold.
