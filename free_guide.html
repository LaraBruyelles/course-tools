What this life actually looks like
Watch first. Then read. This is the job.
Lara — live on board. This is what a typical performance night looks like.

As a solo musician you'll play 2–4 sets per night — piano bars, lounges, outdoor decks — for passengers from around the world. Days are completely free. Meals covered, no rent, no bills. You are, in most ways, treated like a guest who also happens to perform.

The thing that actually makes the difference

Talent gets you interested.
Preparation gets you hired.

Most musicians who apply to cruise ship agencies get rejected — not because they lack talent, but because they don't know the specific preparation required. Agents decide in under sixty seconds. The song list is wrong. The showreel is filmed badly. The email reads like a first draft.

The musicians who land contracts — whether solo pianists, guitar duos, or bands — understand exactly what agencies need to see before they apply. That preparation is a learnable process. And it's exactly what the course teaches.
